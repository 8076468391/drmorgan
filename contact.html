New Patients

What to Bring

  • Photo ID and insurance card
  • Complete list of current medications (including dosages)
  • Medical records from previous physicians
  • Recent test results or imaging reports
  • List of questions or concerns
New Patient Forms Medical History Form

Preparing for Your Visit

  • Arrive 15 minutes early for paperwork
  • Bring a family member or friend for support if desired
  • Note any symptoms you've been experiencing
  • Keep a food and activity diary if recommended
  • Prepare questions about your condition and treatment options

Tip: Write down your questions beforehand to make sure all your concerns are addressed during your visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is a typical appointment?

New patient visits typically last 60-90 minutes, while follow-up appointments are usually 30-45 minutes.

Do you accept walk-in patients?

We primarily see patients by appointment to ensure adequate time for each visit. Please call ahead for availability.

Can I get my test results online?

Yes, we offer a secure patient portal where you can access your test results, appointment history, and communicate with our team.

Cardiac Emergency Information

If you are experiencing chest pain, shortness of breath, palpitations, or other cardiac symptoms, call 911 immediately or go to your nearest emergency room. Do not use this website or contact form for emergency situations.

Nearest Emergency Room: NewYork-Presbyterian Hospital - 525 East 68th Street, New York, NY 10065 - (212) 746-5454

24/7 Cardiac Emergency Line: (212) 555-EMER (3637)

Virtual Visits Available

For established patients, we offer secure telehealth appointments for follow-up visits, medication management, and non-urgent consultations. Virtual visits are conducted via our secure patient portal and are covered by most insurance plans.
